Classic Tiramisu

An elegant Italian dessert that's surprisingly easy to make

Tiramisu is a classic Italian dessert that combines the bold flavors of espresso and mascarpone cheese with the light, airy texture of ladyfingers. This no-bake dessert is perfect for dinner parties or special occasions, and it's always a crowd-pleaser.

Ingredients

  • 6 egg yolks
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 2/3 cup milk
  • 1 1/4 cups heavy cream
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 pound (16 oz) mascarpone cheese, at room temperature
  • 1 3/4 cups strong brewed espresso, cooled
  • 2 tablespoons rum or brandy (optional)
  • 24-30 ladyfingers (savoiardi)
  • 1/4 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 1 oz dark chocolate, for shavings

Instructions

  1. Prepare Custard Base

    In a medium saucepan, whisk together egg yolks and sugar until well blended. Whisk in milk and cook over medium heat, stirring constantly, until the mixture reaches a simmer. Reduce heat to low and cook for 1 minute, still stirring. Remove from heat and allow to cool.

  2. Prepare Cream Mixture

    In a large bowl, whip cream and vanilla until stiff peaks form. In another bowl, whisk mascarpone until smooth, then fold in the cooled custard mixture until well combined. Gently fold in the whipped cream.

  3. Prepare Coffee Mixture

    In a shallow dish, combine espresso and rum (if using).

  4. Assemble

    Quickly dip each ladyfinger into the coffee mixture (don't soak them) and arrange in a single layer in the bottom of a 9x13 inch dish. Spread half of the cream mixture over the ladyfingers.

  5. Add Second Layer

    Repeat with another layer of coffee-dipped ladyfingers and top with the remaining cream mixture.

  6. Finish

    Dust the top with cocoa powder and refrigerate for at least 4 hours or overnight. Before serving, add chocolate shavings on top.

Chef's Notes

For the best flavor, use high-quality espresso and mascarpone. If you prefer a non-alcoholic version, simply omit the rum or brandy. The dessert tastes even better if made a day ahead, allowing the flavors to meld. Make sure to chill thoroughly before serving.
